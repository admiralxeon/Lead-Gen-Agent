# Past Outreach That Won Replies

## To a restaurant with no online ordering
We noticed the business relied only on phone calls and walk-ins, with no way to order or book online. We opened by pointing that out plainly, then referenced our bakery result — over 120 online orders a month — and asked for a short 15-minute call. Being specific about the missing capability, rather than generic praise, is what earned the reply.

## To a business with an outdated mobile site
We led with the observation that most of their visitors were on phones while their site was hard to use on a small screen, which means lost enquiries. We tied it to our law-firm redesign result of roughly 60 percent more mobile enquiries, and offered to show them what a mobile-first version could look like. One concrete observation plus one concrete result works better than listing services.

## General principle that works
The outreach that gets replies names one real problem visible on the prospect's own site, connects it to one relevant result we have delivered, and makes a single low-friction ask. It never uses flattery, never lists every service, and never invents numbers.
